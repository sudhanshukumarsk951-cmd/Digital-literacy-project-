# Responsible Social Media Use: Checklist for College Students

###  The Do’s
1. **Do Audit Your Privacy Settings:** Regularly check who can see your posts on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.
2. **Do Think Before You Post:** Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a professor or future recruiter seeing the content.
3. **Do Fact-Check Before Sharing:** Verify news or academic information from multiple reliable sources before hitting 'Share'.
4. **Do Use Professional Language:** Maintain a level of decorum when engaging in public forums or university-related groups.
5. **Do Give Credit:** Always tag or mention original creators when sharing their work, photos, or research.

###  The Don'ts
1. **Don't Share Sensitive Info:** Never post your VIT registration number, home address, or phone number publicly.
2. **Don't Engage with Trolls:** Avoid heated, disrespectful arguments that could negatively impact your digital reputation.
3. **Don't Post During Lectures:** Focus on academic integrity and stay present during university sessions.
4. **Don't Cyberbully:** Avoid participating in "roasting" or spreading rumors about batchmates or faculty.
5. **Don't Click Suspicious Links:** Avoid "too good to be true" offers or unknown tags that could lead to account phishing.
