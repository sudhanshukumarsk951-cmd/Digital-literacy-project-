 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist: College Student Edition
For college students in India, digital life is integrated into everything from UPI payments at the canteen to submitting assignments on LMS portals. Stay ahead of scammers with these 8 actionable tips:

1.Verify Before You 'Accept' (UPI Safety): Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a stranger or a "buyer" on an unverified marketplace sends you a "Collect Request" or a QR code claiming it's for a refund or payment to you, decline it immediately.

2.Avoid 'Screen Share' for Support (Financial Safety): Technical support for banks or payment apps (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) will never ask you to download apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or RustDesk. These allow fraudsters to see your OTPs and PINs in real-time as you type them.

3.Audit Your App Permissions: Go to your phone settings and check which apps have "SMS" and "Microphone" access. Malicious APKs (like fake "Exam Result" or "Scholarship" trackers) use SMS permissions to silently forward your bank OTPs to hackers.

4.Secure Your 'Edu' Identity: Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on your university email and personal Gmail accounts. This ensures that even if someone phishes your password, they cannot access your academic records or personal data without the second code on your phone.

5.Beware of 'Job' & 'Internship' Scams: Be skeptical of unsolicited WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering "Part-time work-from-home" or "YouTube Like" jobs. If they ask for a "security deposit" or "registration fee" to start working, it is a scam.

6.Use 'Incognito' on Lab Computers: When using library or computer lab systems, never click "Remember Me" or "Save Password." Always log out manually and clear the browser cache before leaving the station to prevent session hijacking.

7.Identify 'Smishing' Links: Do not click on links in SMS messages claiming your "Electricity will be disconnected," "Bank KYC is expired," or "Free 5G Data offer." Always use official apps or websites to check your status.

8.Vigilance on Public Wi-Fi: Avoid conducting banking transactions or entering passwords while connected to free railway or cafe Wi-Fi. If you must use it, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your data traffic.

 How to Report Cybercrime
If you or someone you know has been cheated or harassed online:


Call Immediately: Dial 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline). Acting within the "Golden Hour" (first 2 hours) increases the chances of freezing stolen funds.


Report Online: File a formal complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in.
